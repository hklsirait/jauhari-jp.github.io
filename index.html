Ir. Soekarno

Brief story of "Bung Karno"

Ir. Soekarno was the leader of the independence movement, the father of modern Indonesia and Indonesia’s first president. Known as “Bung Karno well as the Great Leader of the Revolution, Mouthpiece of the Indonesian People and Father of the Farmers, he founded the national ideology Pancasila and the driving force behind Indonesia for the first 17 years of its existence.

He was a prominent leader of Indonesia’s nationalist movement during the Dutch colonial period and spent over a decade under Ducth detention until released by the invading Japanese forces in World War II. Upon Japanese surrender, Soekarno and Mohammad Hatta declared Indonesian independe on 17 August 1945, and Soekarno was appointed as its president. He led Indonesians in resisting Ducth re-colonisation efforts via diplomatic and military means until the Ducth recognition of Indonesian independence in 1949.
